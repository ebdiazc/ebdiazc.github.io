About

I'm a Master's student in Social Policy at the University of Pennsylvania, a Fulbright Scholar, and a Social Justice Scholar. My research focuses on governance, social policy, transparency, and democratic institutions.

Previously, I completed my Bachelor’s degree in Law and Political Science at Tecnológico de Monterrey and worked as a consultant for international organizations such as the United Nations Population Fund, The Pacific Alliance, and the Organization of American States.

Research

My work explores social mobility, transparency, and governance in Latin America. I'm interested in how institutions can improve public policy design through open data and citizen participation.

Publications

I've published research on electoral transparency and access to public information. My latest work won first place in the TEPJF 2024 Essay Contest.
